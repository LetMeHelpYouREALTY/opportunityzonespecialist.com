## Why Property Analysis Matters in Opportunity Zones

Not all opportunity zone properties are created equal. While the tax benefits are standardized, the underlying property fundamentals can vary dramatically. A thorough analysis is essential for maximizing both your tax benefits and investment returns.

## Location and Market Analysis

### Geographic Considerations

**✓ Verify Opportunity Zone Status**
- Confirm the property is within a designated opportunity zone
- Check current zone boundaries (they can change)
- Verify the specific census tract number

**✓ Market Fundamentals**
- Population growth trends in the area
- Employment growth and major employers
- Median household income trends
- Crime rates and safety statistics
- School district quality and ratings

**✓ Transportation and Accessibility**
- Proximity to major highways and public transit
- Walkability scores and bike infrastructure
- Airport accessibility for business travel
- Commute times to major employment centers

### Economic Indicators

**✓ Local Economic Health**
- Unemployment rates compared to state/national averages
- Major employers and their stability
- New business formation rates
- Commercial vacancy rates
- Retail sales trends

**✓ Development Activity**
- Recent and planned development projects
- Infrastructure improvements (roads, utilities, broadband)
- Government incentives and programs
- Zoning changes or planned changes

## Property-Specific Analysis

### Physical Condition Assessment

**✓ Building Inspection**
- Structural integrity and foundation
- Roof condition and age
- HVAC systems and efficiency
- Electrical and plumbing systems
- Environmental concerns (asbestos, lead, mold)

**✓ Development Potential**
- Zoning compliance and potential changes
- Setback requirements and lot coverage
- Parking requirements and availability
- Utility capacity and connections
- Environmental constraints

### Financial Analysis

**✓ Current Income (if applicable)**
- Rental income and occupancy rates
- Operating expenses and maintenance costs
- Property management requirements
- Lease terms and tenant quality

**✓ Development Costs**
- Construction cost estimates
- Permit and approval costs
- Professional fees (architect, engineer, legal)
- Financing costs and interest rates
- Contingency reserves (typically 10-15%)

**✓ Market Comparables**
- Recent sales of similar properties
- Rental rates for comparable properties
- Construction costs per square foot
- Cap rates for similar investments

## Regulatory and Compliance Review

### Opportunity Zone Requirements

**✓ Substantial Improvement Test**
- Ensure planned improvements meet the "substantial improvement" requirement
- Calculate the cost of improvements vs. property basis
- Plan for the 30-month improvement timeline

**✓ Qualified Opportunity Zone Business (QOZB) Requirements**
- Verify the business will meet QOZB requirements
- Review the "50% gross income test"
- Ensure proper use of tangible property

**✓ Compliance Documentation**
- Maintain proper records of all investments
- Document substantial improvement activities
- Track business operations and income

### Legal and Regulatory

**✓ Title and Ownership**
- Clear title and ownership history
- Liens, easements, and encumbrances
- Survey and boundary verification
- Environmental assessments

**✓ Permits and Approvals**
- Required building permits
- Environmental permits and approvals
- Utility connections and approvals
- Zoning variances (if needed)

## Risk Assessment

### Market Risks

**✓ Economic Sensitivity**
- How the property performs in economic downturns
- Diversification of local economy
- Cyclical vs. defensive characteristics

**✓ Competition Analysis**
- Similar projects in the pipeline
- Market saturation risks
- Competitive advantages and disadvantages

### Operational Risks

**✓ Management Complexity**
- Day-to-day management requirements
- Professional management availability
- Tenant relations and turnover

**✓ Maintenance and Capital Expenditures**
- Ongoing maintenance requirements
- Major system replacement schedules
- Capital improvement needs

## Financial Projections

### Pro Forma Analysis

**✓ Revenue Projections**
- Conservative, base, and optimistic scenarios
- Market rent growth assumptions
- Occupancy rate projections
- Additional revenue sources

**✓ Expense Projections**
- Operating expenses and growth rates
- Capital expenditure schedules
- Management fees and professional services
- Insurance and property taxes

**✓ Return Analysis**
- Internal Rate of Return (IRR) calculations
- Net Present Value (NPV) analysis
- Cash-on-cash returns
- Tax benefit calculations

## Exit Strategy Planning

### Long-Term Considerations

**✓ 10-Year Hold Strategy**
- Plan for the full 10-year hold period
- Consider interim exit options
- Tax implications of different exit strategies

**✓ Market Timing**
- Historical market cycles in the area
- Planned infrastructure improvements
- Demographic trends and projections
